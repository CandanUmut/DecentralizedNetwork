You're invited to a community

A small network run entirely by its members — no company, no server, no ads.

What is this?

Every member runs a small program on their own computer. The programs find each other and keep one shared, tamper-proof notebook of who helped whom.

TimeCoin is the unit of that notebook. It cannot be bought or mined — it is created only when a member thanks another member for real help, and each member can give at most … TC of thanks per day. Scarce like time, backed by gratitude.

Messages between members are end-to-end encrypted and travel directly between your computers. Files can be stored with members you trust, paid in TimeCoin. If every server on the internet vanished tomorrow, this community's notebook, messages, and money would keep working.

Join in 3 steps

  1. Install the node on your computer (not this phone): 
    git clone https://github.com/CandanUmut/DecentralizedNetwork
cd DecentralizedNetwork && ./install.sh
  2. Download the join file below and put it next to the program (or send it to yourself — it's just a small text file).
  3. Run: 
    timecoin-node run --data-dir ~/.timecoin --config join.json
    then open http://127.0.0.1:3000 — that's your dashboard. Ask the person who invited you to vouch for you so your coin counts.

Why it's different

Regular apps live on a company's servers: the company sees everything, owns the record, and can change the rules or disappear. Here, you hold your identity (a key on your machine), the record is replicated to every member, money is created by attested help instead of speculation, and trust is a web of real vouches between people who know each other — revocable when it's misplaced. Honest limits: this is young software, not audited, and it works because members trust each other. It is a tool for communities, not a get-rich coin.
